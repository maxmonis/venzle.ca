How to Play

Drag and drop all seven items into the puzzle. Each circle will represent a category, and each item in that circle must fit into that category. This means that the item in the center will be the only one which fits into all three categories, the items in the intersections of two circles will each fit into two of the three, and the items in only one circle will fit into one.

Here is an example:

Canada is in the center because it alone is a constitutional monarchy which touches the arctic circle and has English as an official language. The United States is not a monarchy, Sweden does not have English as an official language, and Australia does not touch the arctic circle. The other three countries each only fit in one category.

It doesn't matter which color you use to represent each category. In the example above, yellow is English, blue is monarchy, and red is the arctic, but the specific colors are arbitrary. All that matters is that one circle contains the four countries which are monarchies, another contains all four which touch the arctic circle, and the final one contains all four where English is officially spoken.
