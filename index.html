Drag and drop all seven items into the puzzle.

How to Play

The four items in each circle each have something in common which the other three items do not. This means that the item in the center will be the only one which fits into all three categories, the items in the intersections of two circles will each fit into two of the three, and the items in only one circle will fit into one.

Here is an example:

South Africa
United States
Canada
Australia
Russia
Sweden
Japan
English is Official Language
Touches the Arctic Circle
Constitutional Monarchy

Canada is in the center because it alone is a constitutional monarchy which touches the arctic circle and has English as an official language. The United States is not a monarchy, Sweden does not have English as an official language, and Australia does not touch the arctic circle. The other three countries each only fit in one category.

In the example above, yellow is English, blue is arctic, and red is monarchy, but the specific colors are arbitrary. All that matters is that one circle contains all four countries which are monarchies, another contains all four which touch the arctic circle, and the final one contains all four where English is officially spoken.

You can make up to three guesses, and have two hints available in case you get stuck (plus a bonus hint once you've used the other two). Try to solve each puzzle using the fewest guesses and hints possible. Good luck and have fun!
